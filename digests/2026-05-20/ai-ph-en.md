## 3. Market Signal

**Agent infrastructure is the overwhelming category concentration**, with 8 of 13 products directly addressing agent orchestration, runtime, triggering, or sandboxing — a clear signal that the market has shifted from "build a chatbot" to "operationalize agent fleets at scale." This infrastructure-heavy pattern suggests the AI product cycle is entering its **platform layer phase**, analogous to cloud computing's evolution past raw VMs into orchestration (Kubernetes) and serverless.

**Innovation is concentrated in operational reliability rather than capability expansion.** The most novel approaches — LobeHub's multi-agent operator model, Agentspan's durability guarantees, AnyFrame's security sandboxing — all solve **production deployment friction**, not model performance. This indicates base models are commoditizing; differentiation now lies in **system design around models**.

**Open-source maintains strategic presence but not dominance.** Only Agentspan is explicitly open-source, though several offer open APIs. The pattern suggests founders are choosing **managed infrastructure plays** with proprietary orchestration layers, betting that developers will pay for reliability over self-hosting complexity. The absence of prominent fine-tuning or model-training tools further confirms that **application and orchestration layers** are where venture-backed builders are placing bets today.
