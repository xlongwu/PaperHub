Developers are not just using AI coding tools—they're systematizing, sharing, and democratizing the *skills* that make these tools effective, creating emergent standards around `.claude` directories and Codex skill formats.

**Memory infrastructure for agents is maturing from novelty to necessity.** [beads](https://github.com/gastownhall/beads), [claude-mem](https://github.com/thedotmack/claude-mem), and [mem0ai/mem0](https://github.com/mem0ai/mem0) collectively signal that context window limitations and session persistence are now primary engineering constraints. The "memory upgrade" framing suggests agent tool vendors may face commoditization of core features, with differentiation moving to memory architectures.

**MCP (Model Context Protocol) is becoming the de facto agent-tool integration standard.** Its appearance across [activepieces](https://github.com/activepieces/activepieces), [langchain4j](https://github.com/langchain4j/langchain4j), and [claude-context](https://github.com/zilliztech/claude-context) indicates rapid ecosystem consolidation around Anthropic's protocol, potentially reducing fragmentation in agent-tool connectivity.
