The category is crowded but differentiated: Mercury Edit 2 focuses on predictive editing, Clovr on frontend generation, OpenGyver on agent orchestration. **Infrastructure innovation is subtle but significant.** OpenRouter's model fusion represents a maturation in how teams consume AI — not betting on single models but dynamically routing for quality. This mirrors the 2024-2025 shift from model-centric to system-centric AI architecture.

**Open-source presence is strategic, not ideological.** Donut Browser and OpenGyver use open source as a trust signal and customization enabler, not a business model. They're competing with closed-source tools on features first, transparency second.

**The "wrapper" critique is outdated.** Today's launches (Fluently, Receipto, Sleek Analytics) embed AI so deeply into workflow-specific UX that the underlying model becomes interchangeable. The value is in integration density, not model access.

**Missing:** No dedicated fine-tuning platforms, no RAG infrastructure plays, no multimodal model hosts. These may be too infrastructure-heavy for Product Hunt's audience, or the market has consolidated around incumbents.
